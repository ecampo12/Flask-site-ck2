Your Character


In this part of the guide we will be looking at your characters. Once you are inside of your game, you will see a circle portrait in the top left corner of your screen. Click on it and it will take you to your character screen.


Your character screen should look similar to the image on the right. As you can see it contains a ton of information. We will go through it section by section. Keep in mind that the character screen is the same for all charcters in the game.



The Left Side

On the right most side of the the character screen you will see a couple of icons. In order, they mean:

  • Ambition: A character's specific personal desire.
  • Demesne: The amount of lanf your character can handle.
  • Army levies: The amount of troops you are able to raise.
  • Vassal Limit: How many vassals you can have.
  • Wealth: How much money you have.
  • Prestige: How famous and notable you are.
  • Piety: How pious your character is.
  • Diplomacy: The number out side the parentheses is your personal diplomacy. That determines your court's personal opinion towards you. The number in the parentheses is your state diplomacy. That determines the opinion pf charcters outside your court, towards you.
  • Martial: The number out side the parentheses is your personal martial. It is used to determine you amry levy and effects how well you command armies. The number in the parentheses is the state martial. It is used to determine the rate at which the character's armies recover morale.
  • Stewardship: Your personal stewardship plus your spouse's stewardship determine the your demesne limit. The state stewardship determines the income of your demesne.
  • Intrigue: The personal intrigue determines how well of a spymaster you would make. The state intrigue increases plot power, makes your plot harder to discover, and increases your imprisonment chances.
  • Learning: The personal learning determins the amount of technology points you gain per month. The state learning gives the charcter piety.
  • Personal Combat: Determines how well your character would do in a duel. Also effects commanding skills.


The Middle Part

The top row shows the your traits. Traits can be earned through out the game. Some traits are good, other bad. The next row show Modifiers. Like traits they offer benefits and penalties, but last only for a limited time. The row underneath that show the titles you own, your claims, and any truces you may have.


The Bottom Half

The bottom half of your character screen has tabs. The family tab lets you view your family. The relationships tab lets you view your relations. The vassal tab lets you view your vassals. The court tab lets you view the characters in your court. The pacts tab lets you view any alliances or defensive pacts you are currently in. Finally, the abroad tab lets you view any family courtiers you have abroad.
